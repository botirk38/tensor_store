# Final Year Project Report - Assessment Marking Grid

**Weighting:** 20% of total project mark

**Word Count:** ~15,000 words (excluding appendices)

---

## Marking Criteria

### 1. Rationale (10 marks)

| % | Description |
|---|---|
| 0–39% | Problem statement or Introduction or Motivation missing or severely underdeveloped. Absence of focus. Tasks unclear or confused. |
| 40–49% | Marginal focus. Relevance of topic explained; problem statement poorly developed. |
| 50–59% | Good relevant introduction. Some shortcomings in clarity of purpose and associated objectives. |
| 60–69% | Clear Motivation and well-written focused introduction that explains the structure of the report and the tasks to be done. |
| 70–100% | Clear statement of problem and associated objectives. Persuasive and comprehensive rationale. Some tasks clearly above second year level. |

### 2. Literature Review and Background Reading (10 marks)

| % | Description |
|---|---|
| 0–39% | No attempt at critical comment; Serious gaps and omissions in literature. No additional material or discussion beyond what was presented in the interim report. |
| 40–49% | Little attempt at critical comment. Large gaps and omissions. |
| 50–59% | Fair knowledge. Some gaps and omissions. Some attempt at critical comment. |
| 60–69% | Sound knowledge of background area. Some critical review. Good understanding. |
| 70–100% | Full critical review of literature relevant to study. Comprehensive knowledge. |

### 3. Contents and Knowledge (20 marks)

| % | Description |
|---|---|
| 0–39% | No evidence of understanding of the project area. Confused conceptual thinking. Poor description of the Software Engineering process. |
| 40–49% | Little evidence of understanding of the project area. Conceptual framework incomplete. Inappropriate or poorly described Software Engineering processes. No additional contents beyond what was demonstrated in the interim report. |
| 50–59% | Evidence of understanding with clear explanations. Adequate coverage. Conceptual framework well developed. Nice clear description of the Software Engineering methodology used. |
| 60–69% | Good knowledgeable account of the project as titled. Ample coverage of the subject matter in sufficient technical detail. Clear explanation of Software Engineering techniques with motivation. |
| 70–100% | Excellent understanding and insight. Conceptual framework underpins study. Expert account of topic. Well thought through Software Engineering content. |

### 4. Technical Decision Making (20 marks)

| % | Description |
|---|---|
| 0–39% | There is no evidence of thought behind choices made. |
| 40–49% | Design Decisions and choice of algorithms justified. No additional decision making beyond that already described in the interim report. |
| 50–59% | Alternatives considered for important technical decisions such as the use of patterns, algorithms or architecture. |
| 60–69% | Clear presentation of technical decisions suggesting that a careful approach has been taken. |
| 70–100% | Technical decisions have been made and presented in way that demonstrates excellent understanding. |

### 5. Critical Analysis and Discussion (20 marks)

| % | Description |
|---|---|
| 0–39% | Weak and unacceptable analysis. Inadequate use of evidence for discussion. No critical evaluation of results. |
| 40–49% | Limited or logically inconsistent analysis. Superficial critical evaluation of results or value of evidence. Analysis is limited to the first term project work. |
| 50–59% | Appropriate critical analysis but limited. Clear presentation of findings. Good analysis of the project process. |
| 60–69% | Clear presentation of findings. Competent analysis. Evidence of ability to evaluate results. Conclusions justified appropriately. |
| 70–100% | High level critical analysis of the process and any deliverables. Clear understanding of the quality of the work. Nice conclusions. |

### 6. Structure, Presentation, and Bibliography (20 marks)

| % | Description |
|---|---|
| 0–39% | Unacceptable layout in terms of structure and logical argument. Inappropriate use of English. Serious deficiencies in presentation and bibliography. |
| 40–49% | Poor layout in terms of structure and logical argument. Bibliography deficient and/or not described and used adequately in the text. |
| 50–59% | Generally good layout and clear literary style. Mainly appropriate presentation. Relevant use of chapter and section structure. Some appropriate figures or tables. Clear well written bibliography. Some citations. |
| 60–69% | Correct, clear English. Clear and competent expression. Consistent layout of the project report. Clear overall structure. Good use of figures. Well formatted bibliography. Citations at correct points in text. |
| 70–100% | Excellent layout. Conforms to all technical specifications. Lucid style of expression in English. Appropriate and innovative presentation. Correct reference to sources and inclusion of a full bibliography. |

### 7. Professional Issues (5 marks)

| % | Description |
|---|---|
| 0–39% | No real attempt to describe any professional issues. |
| 40–49% | Professional issues addressed but poorly thought out and related to the project. |
| 50–59% | Professional issues discussed that are shown to be relevant to the project. |
| 60–69% | Clear discussion of professional issues. Well written and related to project material. |
| 70–100% | Thoughtful discussion of professional issues and how they have affected the project process. |

### Professionalism (assessed separately, 5% of total project mark)

Professionalism is assessed by the supervisor based on the project process, not the report. Relevant evidence in the report includes:
- Evidence of planning and adapting the project plan as priorities shifted
- Reflection on decisions and how the project direction changed
- Documentation of alternative approaches considered and why they were rejected
- Evidence of independent problem-solving and professional conduct

---

## Requirements Summary

- **Word count:** ~15,000 words (excluding appendices)
- **Professional issues chapter:** ~1,000 words, integrated into Chapter 10
- **Appendices:** Acceptable for additional material
- **Total criteria:** 7 categories (80%) + Professionalism (5%) + project demonstration (15%) = 100%
